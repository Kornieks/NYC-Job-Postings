## Dashboard Insights

> Note: The dashboard supports filtering by Salary Frequency (Annual, Daily, Hourly).


### Overview
- The dataset covers **1,399 unique positions** with **2,962 total openings** across **54 agencies** between January and March 2026.
- **57%** of positions require NYC residency.
- Nearly all  positions (**99.82%**) with annual salary are full-time roles. The median annual salary range is **$68.21K – $93.58K**.
- For hourly positions, the median rate is **$17.5 – $19.5/hr**, with 256 unique positions and 905 total openings across 22 agencies.

### Posting Dynamics
- Job postings grew steadily from January, peaking in mid-March at **203 postings** (annual) and **82 postings** (hourly), followed by a sharp drop — likely due to a data collection cutoff.

### Career Level & Salary
- For **annual** positions, the majority target **Experienced (non-manager)** candidates (818 positions, $69.95K – $93.58K).
- For **hourly** positions, **Student** roles dominate (190 positions, $0.02K/hr), followed by Experienced (35) and Entry-Level (30).
- Salary scales with seniority level across both frequency types.

### Job Opportunities by Title
- For **annual** roles: **Social Workers** and **Administrative Support** are most in demand, while **IT Specialists** and **Managers & Directors** offer the highest salaries.
- For **hourly** roles: **Interns & Aides** dominate, followed by **Healthcare** and **Social Workers**.

### Salary Distribution
- **Annual** salaries cluster around **$60K–$100K**, with the most common starting salary at **$64K**.
- **Hourly** rates are highly concentrated at very low K values ($0.01750K – $0.01950K), reflecting part-time and student roles.

### Departments
- For **annual** roles: top hiring departments are **Dept of Health/Mental Hygiene** and **Dept of Environment Protection**.
- For **hourly** roles: **Dept of Parks & Recreation** stands out with only 16 unique positions but **356 total openings** — indicating large-scale bulk hiring for the same role.

### Posting by Weekday
- **Annual** postings peak on Tuesday–Wednesday (227–238).
- **Hourly** postings peak on **Monday** (110), with activity dropping significantly on weekends.
